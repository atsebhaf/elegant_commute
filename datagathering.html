Data Gathering and Domain Analysis

User Analysis

The target user population for the transportation app (Elegant Commute) consists of two classes;

  1. Students in tertiary institutions of education (College and University students) who commute to campus
  2. Middle income earners who commute to work

Students

This class comprises those who have enrolled for higher education and do not have a private means of commuting to campus. They have the following characteristics;

  1. Lack private means of transportation (do not own car)
  2. Require to commute regularly (on average 5 days a week)
  3. Have a tight budget allocation for commuting

b. Middle income earners

This class comprises of people who are required to work onsite but cannot afford to pay for personalised transportation services such as taxis or fuelling their car daily to go to work. They have the following characteristics;

  1. Cannot stably afford private means of transportation (may own a car but cannot afford daily fuel)
  2. Require to commute regularly (on average 5 days a week)
  3. Willing to pay extra for a better transport experience to work

Task Analysis

Logging in: The first step will involve logging into the system. This process ensures that only authorised users can access the platform while at the same time protecting the userâ€™s device from carrying out unauthorised transactions.

Identifying a ride: This is the process of going through the available rides and picking one that is most convenient for the commuter based on the comfort and route time.

Requesting for a ride: This involves asking for a ride in a vehicle chosen by the commuter.

Billing: The process of calculating how much the user is to pay. This amount is based on the distance travelled and the comfort level of the vehicle used.

Feedback: At the end of each ride, the user is prompted to give feedback about their journey and rate their most recent ride.
